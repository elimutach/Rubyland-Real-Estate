Our 8-Step Due Diligence Process

1 Zoning Regulations
2 Neighborhood Analysis
3 SWOT Analysis
4 Highest & Best Use
1

Zoning Regulations

Planning regulations are crucial for the success of any project. At this initial stage, we consider the legal development controls applicable in a certain area getting information on what can be developed and what cannot. This is crucial in defining what change of user can be explored.

2

Neighborhood Analysis

This is where we explore the project's neighborhood assessing how the proposed project would fit. We identify existing gaps that can be exploited and pitfalls that can be avoided. This part of market survey also helps determine the development alternatives prevalent in a given area.

3

SWOT Analysis

We take time to explore the strengths and weaknesses inherent in the proposed project in the light of available alternatives in the market while seeking how the project can benefit from available opportunities and be shielded from potential threats.

4

Highest and Best Use

We advise on the most optimal development that can be carried out subject to constraints imposed by legal and planning laws and in the light of prevailing economic situations.

5

Financing Models

We advise on proposed or available project financing models whether equity or loan finance working out how the same are likely to impact project returns. We also advise if Joint ventures would be the most attractive.

6

Market Assessment

How is the market performing in respect to demand and supply? Is demand effective and sustainable and can we predict future trends based on past and current trends?

7

Project Alternatives

We advise on what other alternatives are available in the market that can be explored further by developing research instruments to identify a latent market need that is yet to be explored.

8

Risk Assessment

We identify potential risks legal or financial including credit risk that are likely to face the proposed project and develop measures on how the same can be mitigated.
