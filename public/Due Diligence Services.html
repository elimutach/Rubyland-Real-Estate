Why Due Diligence Matters

Due diligence is a comprehensive verification process conducted before purchasing or investing in property to confirm legal ownership, identify risks, and protect client capital.

In the Kenyan property market, title irregularities, boundary disputes, encumbrances, and survey discrepancies are common risk factors. Investors and institutions need authoritative due diligence to avoid costly legal and financial exposure.

Warning: Without proper due diligence, you risk purchasing disputed properties, properties with hidden encumbrances, or properties that cannot be developed as intended.

What We Do

1

Title Search Verification

Comprehensive title search at the Lands Registry to verify legal ownership and authenticity of property documents. We confirm the seller has legal capacity to transfer the property and identify any existing caveats or restrictions.

Our team examines historical records to ensure clean title transfer and identifies any potential ownership disputes or competing claims.

2

Registry & Survey Checks

Thorough examination of registry records and survey plans to confirm property boundaries, dimensions, and physical characteristics. We verify that the property matches the description in sale documents.

Survey record checks identify any boundary disputes, encroachments, or discrepancies between the actual property and official records.

3

Encumbrance Identification

Identification of all encumbrances, restrictions, and charges affecting the property. This includes mortgages, liens, easements, rights of way, and any other limitations on property use or transfer.

We assess the impact of these encumbrances on your intended use of the property and provide recommendations for addressing them.

4

Risk Analysis & Mitigation

Comprehensive risk analysis identifying potential legal, financial, and regulatory risks associated with the property purchase. We evaluate environmental concerns, zoning restrictions, and development limitations.

Our team provides specific risk mitigation recommendations and strategies to protect your investment and ensure a smooth transaction process.

Title Irregularities

One of the most common risks in Kenyan property transactions is title irregularities including fake title deeds, forged documents, and improper transfers. Our thorough verification process at the Lands Registry confirms document authenticity and proper registration history.

We examine the entire chain of title to ensure each transfer was properly executed and registered, protecting you from purchasing disputed or illegally acquired property.
